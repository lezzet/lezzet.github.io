About Böreks

Known as burek in Slavic countries, boureki or spanakopita in Greece, bourekas in Isreal, or boureg in Armenian, böreks are truly an international dish.

Böreks are a family of baked filled pastries made of a thin flaky dough known as phyllo (or yufka), of Anatolian origins and also found in the cuisines of the Balkans, Levant, Mediterranean, and other countries in Eastern Europe and Western Asia.

A börek may be prepared in a large pan and cut into portions after baking, or as individual pastries.

We serve tray böreks (also known as tepsi böreği) that come wrapped in triangles in a tray and are made with phyllo dough.
